# KeyVox Brand & Trademark Use Policy

Version: 1.0
Owner: Dominic Esposito
Project: KeyVox

## 1) Purpose

This policy defines permitted and restricted uses of the KeyVox name and brand identity.

## 2) Code License vs. Trademark Rights

- KeyVox source code is licensed under MIT (see `LICENSE.md`).
- Trademark and brand permissions are separate from source-code license permissions.
- No trademark rights are granted through the MIT license.

## 3) Covered Marks and Brand Elements

This policy applies to:

- The name `KeyVox`
- KeyVox logos, app icons, and related brand imagery
- Brand-identity assets listed as excluded/proprietary in `LICENSE.md`, including:
  - `macOS/Resources/Assets.xcassets/`
  - `macOS/Resources/keyvox.icon/`
  - `macOS/Resources/logo.png`
  - `macOS/Views/Components/KeyVoxLogo.swift`
  - `macOS/Views/Components/LogoBarView.swift`

## 4) Allowed Uses (No Prior Approval Required)

You may:

- Make factual references such as "fork of KeyVox" or "based on KeyVox"
- Use nominative references for compatibility, origin, or discussion
- Link to this repository

These references must be accurate and must not imply endorsement or official status.

## 5) Restricted Uses (Require Written Permission)

You may not, without prior written permission:

- Use `KeyVox` as the product, distribution, or company name for a fork
- Use KeyVox logos/icons/brand visuals in redistributed or commercial builds
- Use branding that is confusingly similar to KeyVox
- Represent a fork as official, affiliated, certified, or endorsed by KeyVox

## 6) Fork and Redistribution Requirements

When redistributing or commercializing a fork of the MIT-licensed code, you must:

1. Rename your application
2. Remove and replace KeyVox-proprietary brand assets listed in `LICENSE.md`
3. Use distinct branding, iconography, and visual identity
4. Avoid market-facing presentation that could cause confusion with KeyVox

## 7) Attribution and Naming Guidance

Acceptable examples:

- "Forked from KeyVox"
- "Built from the MIT-licensed KeyVox source code"

Not acceptable without permission:

- "Official KeyVox build"
- Product naming that suggests it is KeyVox or an official KeyVox edition

## 8) No Implied Endorsement

Using KeyVox source code does not create partnership, sponsorship, endorsement, or certification rights.

## 9) Permission Requests

For trademark or branding permissions, contact the project owner in writing.

## 10) Relationship to LICENSE.md

This policy complements `LICENSE.md`. If language differs, `LICENSE.md` controls repository licensing scope.
